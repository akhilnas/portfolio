The increasing ubiquity of Graph Networks in Deep Learning for solving various problems leads us to the question of generating Graph Networks to analyze the behaviour of these systems.

In this part, we will look at the most basic of Graph Generative models, namely the Erdős–Rényi Random Graph Model.

## Erdős–Rényi Model

The model simply follows the Bernoulli Distribution for each possible edge between two nodes:

$$A_{ij} \sim \text{Bernoulli}(p)$$

Here $A_{ij}$ denotes the adjacency matrix entry with respect to nodes $i$ and $j$.

## Drawbacks

Being the simplest generative model, this comes with a lot of drawbacks.

### 1. Degree Distribution

The degree distribution of the model follows a Poisson distribution, whereas all real-world data follow a power-law distribution.

### 2. Diameter of Graph

The diameter of the graphs generated by the model works out to $\frac{\log(N)}{\log(p(N-1))}$, where:

- $p$: the probability of an edge occurring
- $N$: the total number of nodes of the graph

### 3. Clustering Coefficient

The clustering coefficient is the same as the probability of an edge occurring. This implies that there are no communities of nodes being formed, which deviates from real-world graphs.

All of these imply that this generative model is unable to accurately capture the density of real-world graphs.
